Obesity

Causes, Symptoms, & Treatment



What is obesity?

Obesity is a chronic health condition involving excess body fat that raises the risk for heart disease — the leading cause of death in the United States — and is linked to many other health problems, including type 2 diabetes and cancer. Nearly 3 in 4 adults age 20 or older in the United States have either overweightness or obesity. Nearly 1 in 5 children and teens ages 2 to 19 years have obesity. Overweightness and obesity can lead to serious health issues for people of all ages.

What causes obesity?

Obesity results when someone regularly takes in more calories than needed. The body stores these excess calories as body fat, and over time the extra pounds add up. There are a large amount of factors that can lead to obesity, such as diet and lifestyle factors, low physical activity, poor sleep, genetics, and even medical reasons.

 


Obesity Symptoms

People with obesity can suffer from a wide variety of complications. Skin problems, shortness of breath, trouble sleeping, fatigue, and even psychological issues such as negative self-esteem and depression can come as a result of obesity. Obesity can also increase your risk of developing many potentially serious health conditions, including type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, asthma, liver and kidney disease, cancer, etc.

 


How do you treat obesity?

The best way to treat obesity is to exercise regularly and to eat a healthy, reduced-calorie diet. To do this, you can: eat a balanced calorie-controlled diet as recommended by a GP or weight loss management health professional (such as a dietitian)
take up activities such as fast walking, jogging, swimming or tennis for 150 to 300 minutes (2.5 to 5 hours) a week
You may also benefit from receiving support and counselling from a trained healthcare professional to help you better understand your relationship with food and develop different eating habits.
